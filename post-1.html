Global Market Forecast: What to Expect in Q4 2026

David Chen
•Oct 22, 2026• 6 min read

As we transition into the final quarter of 2026, the global market continues to experience significant realignments driven by technological advancements and shifting central bank policies. Following a somewhat tumultuous mid-year, institutional investors are resetting their expectations, eyeing opportunities across both traditional equities and emerging asset classes.

Interest Rate Shifts

The Federal Reserve's pivot towards a more accommodating stance has had a cascading effect globally. As inflation metrics stabilize within target ranges across major economies, central banks in Europe and Asia are similarly adjusting their baseline rates. This coordinated easing is providing much-needed liquidity, primarily benefiting growth sectors that have been traditionally sensitive to high borrowing costs.

"The synchronized shift in monetary policy globally marks an inflection point. The liquidity premium is returning, and we expect capital reallocation towards undervalued tech and infrastructure." — FTLuma Market Analysis Team

Emerging Markets and AI Infrastructure

A persistent theme going into Q4 is the ongoing investment boom in infrastructure relative to Artificial Intelligence (AI). Beyond software, the actual physical infrastructure—data centers, green energy suppliers, and advanced semiconductor manufacturing—remains heavily favored. Emerging markets that supply essential raw materials for these industries are seeing unprecedented inflows.

At FTLuma, we view this as a multi-year cyclical uptrend rather than a temporary spike. Investors holding long positions in these structural growth areas will likely outpace those rotating back into defensive stocks too early.
